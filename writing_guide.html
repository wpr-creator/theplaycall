The Play Call · Writing Guide

Choose Your Voice

For this assignment, you will write about the same fiscal policy event in two different ways: one creative piece and one analytical piece.

Your Task

One Event. Two Writing Styles.

Choose ONE creative style and ONE analytical style. Both pieces must focus on the same event from The Play Call website. Each piece should be four paragraphs and should explain how the policy worked, who benefited, and who paid the cost.

Creative Choice
Fiction

Write a realistic scene with a character living through the policy. Show the impact through their experience.

Paragraph 1Set the scene. Who is your character? Where are they?
Paragraph 2Show the policy in action. What changes around them?
Paragraph 3Reveal the impact. How does the policy help or hurt them?
Paragraph 4Connect to the bigger idea. Who wins, and who pays?
Creative Choice
Satire

Use humor, exaggeration, or irony to reveal something unfair or absurd about the policy.

Paragraph 1Introduce the fake situation, speech, ad, or scene.
Paragraph 2Exaggerate who benefits from the policy.
Paragraph 3Expose the hidden cost or unfair outcome.
Paragraph 4Make the point clear through irony or contrast.
Analytical Choice
Informative

Explain how the policy worked and what it cost. Stay neutral and focus on clear explanation.

Paragraph 1Explain the crisis or problem the government faced.
Paragraph 2Explain what the government did using fiscal policy.
Paragraph 3Explain the seen benefit and who gained from it.
Paragraph 4Explain the hidden cost and who paid for it.
Analytical Choice
Argument

Take a position on whether the intervention was worth it and defend your claim with evidence.

Paragraph 1State your claim: was the policy worth it or not?
Paragraph 2Use evidence to explain the strongest benefit.
Paragraph 3Use evidence to explain the biggest cost.
Paragraph 4Defend your final judgment: did the benefit outweigh the cost?

Vocabulary Requirement: Use at least two lesson terms in each writing piece.

Your terms should carry meaning. Do not just drop them into a sentence. Use them to explain the policy, the benefit, or the cost.
